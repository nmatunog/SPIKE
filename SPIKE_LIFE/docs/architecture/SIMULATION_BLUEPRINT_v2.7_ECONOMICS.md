SPIKE LIFE™
Simulation Blueprint v2.7
Economic Model, Balancing Rules & Progression Tuning
Version: 2.7
Purpose:
Define the economic model that governs the SPIKE LIFE simulation.
This blueprint ensures:
* Financial realism
* Educational integrity
* Balanced gameplay
* Meaningful decision-making
* Long-term sustainability
It establishes the "laws of the SPIKE LIFE economy."

1. Design Philosophy
The economy exists to teach financial planning.
It does not exist to reward luck or punish players unfairly.
Every economic rule should reinforce one or more SPIKE learning objectives:
* Cash Flow Management
* Financial Needs Analysis
* Protection Planning
* Goal Planning
* Financial Entrepreneurship
* Retirement Readiness

2. Core Economic Principles
Principle 1
Cash Flow drives everything.
Income alone does not determine success.

Principle 2
Protection reduces financial volatility.

Principle 3
Goals compete for limited resources.

Principle 4
Debt creates leverage and risk.

Principle 5
Time is the greatest wealth multiplier.

9. Protection Economics
Protection should never feel like a penalty.
Instead:
Protection reduces downside volatility.
Players with stronger protection plans should experience:
Lower financial losses
Faster recovery
Higher long-term stability

This mirrors real financial planning.

14. Progression Tuning
Difficulty increases through:
More responsibilities
More variables
Greater financial commitments
Not through artificially larger penalties.

Medical Event

Age 42

Mortgage

15. Failure States
SPIKE LIFE does not end because of one bad year.
Instead:
Players enter Recovery Mode.
Recovery requires:
Positive Cash Flow
Debt Reduction
Rebuilding Protection
Rebuilding Goals
This reinforces resilience.

16. Success States
The simulation has multiple successful outcomes.
Examples
Financially Secure Family
Successful Entrepreneur
Prepared Retiree
Balanced Planner
Community Leader
No single path defines success.

17. Randomness Policy
Randomness should create uncertainty.
It should never determine outcomes by itself.
Target Influence
Events
≈30%
Player Decisions
≈70%
This keeps learning centered on decision-making.

18. Educational Balance Rules
Every life stage must expose:
Cash Flow decisions
Protection decisions
Goal decisions
Long-term planning decisions
No stage should overemphasize investing or entrepreneurship.

19. Exploit Prevention
Players should not be able to exploit the simulation through repetitive actions.
Examples
Repeatedly choosing only high-risk investments
Repeatedly delaying protection
Accumulating unrealistic debt
Ignoring retirement indefinitely
The engine should surface increasingly severe long-term consequences.

20. AI Recommendation Guardrails
Future AI-generated recommendations must always follow this priority hierarchy:
1. Maintain Positive Cash Flow
2. Build Emergency Fund
3. Close Critical Protection Gaps
4. Manage Unsustainable Debt
5. Fund Essential Goals
6. Build Long-Term Wealth
7. Optimize Lifestyle Choices
This hierarchy is non-negotiable and reflects the educational philosophy of SPIKE LIFE.

22. Balancing Validation Checklist
Before every release, validate:
* Positive cash flow is achievable but requires discipline.
* Emergency funds meaningfully reduce hardship.
* Protection plans measurably reduce financial losses.
* Goal funding requires tradeoffs.
* Retirement cannot be ignored without consequence.
* Entrepreneurship offers upside but also meaningful risk.
* No single dominant strategy exists.
* Multiple financially responsible paths can succeed.

23. Final Design Guardrail
Whenever a new feature is proposed, evaluate it against these questions:
1. Does it teach a SPIKE learning objective?
2. Does it reinforce Financial Needs Analysis?
3. Does it create a meaningful financial decision?
4. Does it improve advisory thinking?
5. Does it preserve long-term balance?
If the answer to any of these is "No," the feature should be redesigned or excluded from the MVP.
